## **2.3.3 Anti-Patterns — What to Avoid**

**Section Overview (25–50 words):**  
Anti-patterns expose practices that reduce clarity, consistency, or reliability in template systems. Avoiding them ensures stable performance and maintainability.  
**When to Use It:** When reviewing templates for quality or debugging issues.  
**Mini-Note:** Avoiding anti-patterns preserves long-term system health.

|**Anti-Pattern**|**Description (15–20 words)**|
|---|---|
|**Overtemplating**|Excessive structure restricts creativity and adaptability.|
|**Loose Variable Names**|Causes confusion and mismatched placeholder behavior.|
|**Mixed Instruction Styles**|Inconsistent tone or structure confuses LLMs.|
|**Hardcoded Values**|Reduces reusability and creates maintenance burden.|
|**No Input Validation**|Allows malformed data to corrupt rendering.|
|**Template Sprawl**|Too many similar templates without proper organization.|

- Mix flashcards from different topics
- Solve problems requiring multiple concepts
- Compare and contrast related ideas

# ADDITIONAL INSTRUCTIONS FOR AI:

1. **Adapt to subject type**: If it's a language, focus on conversation practice. If it's theory, focus on understanding principles. If it's technical skills, focus on hands-on projects.

2. **Scale appropriately**: For simple topics (e.g., "Git basics"), make this concise. For complex topics (e.g., "Machine Learning"), expand each section.

3. **Make it actionable**: Every section should have clear next steps, not just information.

4. **Include examples**: Use real examples, not placeholders like "[example here]".

5. **Progressive complexity**: Ensure each level genuinely builds on the previous one.

6. **Cognitive load awareness**: Don't overwhelm with too many concepts per session. Stick to 5-7 new items maximum.

7. **Use the Feynman principle**: Explain things simply enough that a motivated beginner could follow.

8. **Practical focus**: Prioritize what I'll actually use over comprehensive theory.
